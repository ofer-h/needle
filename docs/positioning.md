# Positioning & Pitch

**Product:** Needle (working name: Focus)
**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Purpose:** The plain-language way to describe and pitch Needle — what it is, what
makes it different, and how to talk about it when you meet a relevant person.

---

## The core idea (one line)

> Every other app helps you **plan**. Needle helps you **let go of one task and
> start the next** — the exact moment focus is lost.

Needle is **not a to-do app** and **not a timer**. It is a **transition coach**:
it catches you at the moment of the switch, helps you clear your head, and points
you at the next thing. That contrast is the sharpest way to position it — lead
with it.

The mechanic that makes it work: it **rotates how it interrupts you**, so your
brain never learns to ignore it. Novelty is the antidote to habituation — the
brain gets bored of any single trick, so the tool keeps surprising it.

---

## The pitch, in three sizes

### One-liner (say it fast)

> "Needle helps you switch from one task to the next without carrying the last
> one in your head."

### 15 seconds (when you meet someone)

> "You know that moment when one meeting ends and the next one starts, but your
> head is still stuck in the last one? Needle catches you right there. It stops
> you for ten seconds, makes you dump what's on your mind, and you walk into the
> next thing clear. And it keeps changing *how* it interrupts you, so your brain
> never learns to ignore it."

### Full version (doc / landing page / investor)

> For people who lose time and focus every time they switch tasks or meetings,
> **Needle is a Mac app that coaches you through the switch itself.** It
> interrupts you at the hard stop, makes you let go of the last thing, and starts
> you clean on the next. Unlike to-do lists and timers — which only help you
> *plan* — Needle acts at the **transition**, the moment the brain breaks down.
> And because it rotates how it nudges you, it never becomes background noise.

This follows the standard elevator template:
*for [who] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [benefit], unlike
[alternative], because [differentiator].*

## How to pitch it in person

Five rules, kept simple:

1. **Start with the pain, not the feature.** Open with "you know that moment
   when…" — let them nod before you say what you built. Never open with "it's an
   app that…".
2. **One person, one sentence.** Don't list features. The interrupt, the
   brain-dump, the calendar trigger — that's *how*, not *what*. Cut it from the
   first 30 seconds.
3. **Say what it's NOT.** "It's not another to-do app" sticks, because everyone
   has to-do-app fatigue.
4. **End with a question, not a close.** "Does that happen to you?" gets them
   talking. The goal is a second conversation, not a sale.
5. **Keep your "why me" in your back pocket.** If they lean in: *"I've worked
   with an ADHD coach for years — the brain gets bored of any trick, so you have
   to keep surprising it. That's the whole product."* No competitor can say that.

## What makes it different (the white space)

The market splits into three buckets. None of them own the moment of the switch:

| Category | Examples | What it does | What it misses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Timers / timeboxing | Llama Life, Tiimo | "What am I doing now, for how long" — *you* set it | Doesn't act *at* the transition; you have to remember to use it |
| Body doubling | Focusmate, Centered | Someone works alongside you for accountability | Needs scheduling; not about the switch itself |
| Daily planners | Sunsama, Akiflow, Morgen | Guided planning ritual once a day, then a list | One ritual a day, not every transition |
| OS-level | Apple Focus / Reduce Interruptions | *Silences* notifications | The opposite — it hides interruptions instead of using one on purpose |

The "transition ritual" (stop, close the loop, start clean) is **everywhere as
advice** but **nobody has productized it.** No tool actively catches you *at* the
hard stop, makes you dump your head, and **rotates the intervention so it doesn't
go stale.** That rotation is the defensible idea.

Full competitor breakdown: see [`competitive-landscape.md`](./competitive-landscape.md).

### The obvious objection — and the answer

**"Why won't I just set a timer / a reminder?"**
A timer becomes wallpaper within a week — the brain habituates and stops seeing
it. The rotating kit is built specifically so that never happens. That's the moat,
not a feature.

## Open decision: the name

The docs flip between **"Needle"** and **"Focus"**:

- **"Focus"** is descriptive but crowded — nearly every app in the table above
  calls itself a focus app.
- **"Needle"** is distinctive and carries the "move the needle / am I on the most
  important thing" meaning, which is also the product's check-in prompt.

Leaning Needle, but still open.
