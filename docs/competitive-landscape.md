**Source:** Repo analysis + market research

## What Needle Is

Needle is a macOS app that **coaches you through the moment you switch from one
task (or meeting) to the next** — the moment focus is lost. It:

1. **Interrupts** you at the hard stop — when one focus block ends and another begins.
2. **Clears your head** — a quick brain-dump to let go of the last thing.
3. **Starts you clean** on the next thing.

The defensible mechanic: Needle **rotates how it interrupts you** so the brain
never habituates to it. Novelty is the antidote to habituation — variety is what
keeps the nudge working past week one.

Needle is **not a to-do app** and **not a timer**. It is a **transition coach**.
The brain-dump's value is the *act* of dumping, not a stored, searchable record —
so capture/retrieval is deliberately thin, not the product.

---

## Needle's Differentiation

- **Acts at the transition.** Every other tool helps you *plan ahead* or *capture
  after*. Needle does its work at the switch itself — the moment of attention
  residue, when the last task is still in your head.
- **It interrupts on purpose.** Most tools (and the OS) try to *reduce*
  interruptions. Needle uses one well-timed interruption as the intervention.
- **Rotating kit, not one trick.** It varies the intervention so it doesn't become
  wallpaper. This is the moat — a static timer or reminder habituates within a week.
- **macOS-native, local-first.** Global shortcut, calendar-aware, no cloud required.
- **Calm, not aggressive.** A coach for the transition, not a streak/shame machine.

## Closest Competitors (by the job they do)

The market splits into buckets. **None of them own the moment of the switch.**

### Tier 1 — Focus-state intervention (closest thesis: act on you in the moment)

| Company / product | Overlap | Where Needle differs |
|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **ADHD Focus Mate** (macOS menu bar) | AI watches focus, nudges on distraction | Needle fires at *transitions*, not continuous distraction-policing; adds the let-go/brain-dump ritual |
| **[Centered](https://www.centered.app)** | Distraction detection during a session | Session-bound coworking; doesn't coach the switch between sessions |
| **[Brain.fm](https://www.brain.fm)** | Publishes the "2-minute transition ritual" science; audio to shift states | Content + audio, not an app that *enforces* the transition for you |

This tier is the closest by mechanism — but the **transition ritual itself is
advice, not a product.** That gap is Needle's white space.

### Tier 2 — Timers / timeboxing (you set it; it counts down)

| Company / product | Overlap | Where Needle differs |
|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **[Llama Life](https://llamalife.co)** | "What am I doing now, for how long"; single-tasking | *You* start it; no transition coaching; static, habituates |
| **[Tiimo](https://www.tiimoapp.com)** | Visual timeline, countdowns, task breakdown | Planning/structure tool; doesn't interrupt at the switch |

Needle acts *at the boundary* a timer only marks — and rotates, so it doesn't go stale.

### Tier 3 — Body doubling / presence

| Company / product | Overlap | Where Needle differs |
|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **[Focusmate](https://www.focusmate.com)** | Scheduled coworking; the session is the ritual | Needs a booked partner; not about the moment of switching |
| **[Centered](https://www.centered.app)**, **[Tiimo](https://www.tiimoapp.com)** (async body double) | Companionship for task initiation | Solves *getting started*, not *letting go and switching* |

### Tier 4 — Daily planners / AI scheduling (plan once, then a list)

| Company / product | Overlap | Where Needle differs |
|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **[Sunsama](https://sunsama.com)** | Guided daily planning *ritual* | One ritual per day, then a list — not a per-transition coach |
| **[Akiflow](https://akiflow.com)** | Unified inbox, time-blocking | Keyboard-first manual planner; user triages |
| **[Morgen](https://www.morgen.so)** | Calendar + tasks in one view | Consolidation, not in-the-moment intervention |
| **[Motion](https://www.usemotion.com)**, **[Reclaim.ai](https://reclaim.ai)** | AI auto-schedules tasks into the calendar | Optimizes the *plan*; silent at the actual switch |

### Tier 5 — OS-level focus

| Company / product | Overlap | Where Needle differs |
|-------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **Apple Focus / Reduce Interruptions** (macOS, iOS) | Filters/silences notifications by context | Does the **opposite** — hides interruptions; Needle uses one on purpose |

### Tier 6 — Capture / "second brain" (adjacent; was Needle's older thesis)

| Company / product | Overlap | Note |
|-------------------|---------|------|
| **[Saner.AI](https://saner.ai)**, **[Pith](https://www.trypithapp.com)** | ADHD brain-dump → tasks, proactive check-ins | Compete on *capture + organize*; Needle's dump is ephemeral, the ritual is the point |
| **Notion**, **Todoist**, **Apple Reminders**, **Obsidian** | Store/organize tasks & notes | Different job — structure and retrieval, not the transition |

These were front-and-center under the old capture/second-brain framing. Under the
transition-coach thesis they are **adjacent**, not direct competitors.

## Competitive Position

The open space is **"acts at the moment of the switch"** — and nobody is there.

```
                  Acts AT the transition (in the moment)
                              ↑
                              |
       ADHD Focus Mate ●      |      ● Needle
            Centered ●        |
                              |
   ← Reduce / silence ————————+———————— Use an interruption on purpose →
     (Apple Focus ●)          |
                              |
       Llama Life ●  Sunsama ●|● Akiflow   ● Saner.AI / Pith
        Motion ● Reclaim ●    |
                              ↓
                  You plan / capture ahead of time
```

Needle aims for the **top-right quadrant**: it deliberately interrupts you *at the
transition*. The crowded competition lives in the bottom row (plan/capture ahead)
or the bottom-left (silence interruptions). The "transition ritual" is widely
preached as a technique but not productized — that is the wedge.

**Direct competitors to watch:** ADHD Focus Mate, Centered (distraction
detection), and any timer/planner that adds an active in-the-moment transition
intervention (Llama Life, Tiimo, Sunsama are the most likely to drift toward it).

**Indirect competitors** (users may use them for parts of the job): Llama
Life/Tiimo for timers, Focusmate for accountability, Sunsama/Akiflow/Motion for
planning, Saner.AI/Pith for capture.

## The Obvious Objection

**"Why won't I just set a timer or a reminder?"**
A static timer becomes wallpaper within a week — the brain habituates and stops
seeing it. Needle's rotating kit is built specifically so that never happens.
That's the moat, and it's also why the existing timer/reminder products in Tiers
2 and 5 don't actually solve this job.
